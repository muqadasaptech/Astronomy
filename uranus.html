Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun, and the third largest planet in our solar system.
It appears to spin sideways.

Introduction

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is often known as an “ice giant” due to its cold and icy composition. It was discovered in 1781 by William Herschel, making it the first planet discovered with a telescope.

Size

Diameter of approximately 50,724 kilometers, making it the third-largest planet in our solar system.

Temperature

Extremely cold, with average temperatures around -224°C, making it one of the coldest planets.

Potential for life

Scientists don't expect to find living things currently thriving on Mars. Instead, they're looking for signs of life that existed long ago, when Mars was warmer and covered with water.

Ring

Has a faint ring system composed mostly of dark particles and dust. These rings are much fainter and less extensive than Saturn's.

Moons

Has 27 known moons, with major moons including Titania, Oberon, Ariel, Umbriel, and Miranda. Most of these moons are icy and cratered..

Formation

Formed about 4.5 billion years ago, likely farther out in the solar system than their current positions, and migrated to their current orbits. Their icy compositions reflect their formation in a colder part of the solar nebula.

Atmosphere

Primarily composed of hydrogen, helium, and methane. Methane gives Uranus a blue-green color by absorbing red light and scattering blue light.

Surface

Has no solid surface as it is a gas giant. The outer layer consists of gas and ice, while beneath lies a dense layer of water, ammonia, and methane.

Structure

Has a small rocky core surrounded by an icy mantle made of water, ammonia, and methane. Above the icy mantle lies the thick gaseous atmosphere.
