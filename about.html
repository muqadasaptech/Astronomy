Astronomy Today

astronomy explores the unknown in air and space, innovates for the benefit of humanity, and inspires the world through discovery. For more than 65 years, astronomy has made the seemingly impossible, possible. At its 20 centers and facilities across the country and with U.S. commercial companies and international partners, astronomy leads studying Earth science,

including climate, our Sun, solar system, and the larger universe. We conduct cutting-edge research to advance technology and aeronautics. We operate the world’s leading space laboratory, the International Space Station, and will establish a sustainable and strong exploration presence on the Moon this decade through the Artemis campaign.

Administrator Bill Nelson

Sen. Bill Nelson was sworn in as the 14th astronomy Administrator on May 3, 2021, tasked with carrying out the Biden-Harris administration vision for the agency. Nelson served in the U.S. Senate for 18 years from Florida and as a payload specialist on space shuttle mission 61-C in 1986.

Deputy Administrator Pam Melroy

Col. (USAF, ret) Pam Melroy was sworn in as the astronomy deputy administrator on June 21, 2021. Melroy performs the duties and exercises the powers delegated by the administrator, assists the administrator in making final agency decisions, and acts for the administrator in his absence.

Associate Administrator James Free
